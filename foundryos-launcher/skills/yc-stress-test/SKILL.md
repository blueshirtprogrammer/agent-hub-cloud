# YC-Style Stress Test Skill

## Purpose

Use this skill to attack a startup, company template, or GTM plan before the market does.

The goal is not encouragement.

The goal is clarity.

## Trigger

Use when asked to review:

- FOUNDRYOS Launcher,
- a company template,
- a pitch,
- a demo,
- a pricing model,
- a GTM plan,
- an investor narrative,
- a new autonomous company idea.

## Core Question

```text
Is this a real business, or an impressive AI demo?
```

## Stress Test Dimensions

Score each 1-5.

### 1. Problem urgency

Ask:

- Who is in pain right now?
- Why is the pain expensive?
- Why would they pay this month?
- What do they currently do instead?

Fail if the answer is vague or horizontal.

### 2. Buyer clarity

Ask:

- Who signs?
- Who uses?
- Who blocks?
- Who owns budget?
- Can we list 100 targets this week?

Fail if the buyer is “businesses.”

### 3. Wedge sharpness

Ask:

- What is the first narrow use case?
- Can a buyer understand it in 10 seconds?
- Does it expand into the full vision later?

Fail if it leads with the whole grand theory.

### 4. Demo credibility

Ask:

- Can the demo show real workflow motion?
- Does it create artifacts?
- Does it show proof-of-work?
- Does it stop at approval gates?

Fail if the demo is only a pretty dashboard.

### 5. Revenue proximity

Ask:

- What can be sold this week?
- What is the first paid pilot?
- What is the setup fee?
- Can payment happen before full product maturity?

Fail if the plan depends on scale before cash.

### 6. Distribution access

Ask:

- Which channel reaches buyers now?
- What warm network exists?
- Who can resell this?
- What proof asset creates pull?

Fail if distribution is “content and ads later.”

### 7. Pricing strength

Ask:

- Is pricing aligned to transformation value?
- Is it too cheap for the required support?
- Does it create a premium signal?

Fail if unlimited usage is sold cheaply.

### 8. Trust and governance

Ask:

- Are external actions approval-gated?
- Are credentials handled legitimately?
- Is proof visible?
- Can workflows be stopped or rolled back?

Fail if it relies on hidden automation, account abuse, or unverifiable actions.

### 9. Moat compounding

Ask:

- What improves with every customer?
- What data, skills, templates, or workflows compound?
- What gets harder to copy after 50 installs?

Fail if the moat is “better prompts.”

### 10. Investor narrative

Ask:

- Why can this become a platform?
- What repeats across customers?
- Why now?
- What does scale unlock?

Fail if it sounds like a service agency only.
