# FOUNDRYOS™ GTM Plan

## GTM doctrine

Do not launch as a generic AI agent platform.

Launch with one narrow proof:

> **We deployed an AI company that can sell and deliver an enterprise hospitality AI offer.**

Specific beats generic.

Proof beats hype.

Revenue beats roadmap.

## First ICPs

### ICP 1: AI agencies and automation consultants

They already sell automations but lack enterprise-grade company packaging.

Pain:

- too much custom work,
- weak delivery consistency,
- no governance layer,
- poor proof-of-work reporting,
- hard to productise services.

Offer:

> Lease a full AI company setup that sells, scopes, proposes, delivers, and reports in your niche.

### ICP 2: Hospitality consultants and operators

They have access to hotels and venues but lack technical execution depth.

Offer:

> Deploy an AI hospitality revenue company that sells guest upsell, concierge, minibar, procurement, and venue automation.

### ICP 3: Venture studios and startup builders

They want repeatable validation infrastructure.

Offer:

> Use FOUNDRYOS to stand up validated venture shells with agents, workflows, GTM, reporting, and capital readiness.

### ICP 4: Enterprise innovation teams

They want governed AI deployment, not random staff using random tools.

Offer:

> Deploy internal AI operating companies with approval gates, audit, and proof-of-work reporting.

## Kill criteria

Kill or pivot any segment that cannot produce:

- a booked call within 100 targeted outreaches,
- a paid pilot within 10 serious conversations,
- a repeatable pain statement,
- a willingness to pay for setup or template access.
