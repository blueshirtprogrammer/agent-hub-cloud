# FOUNDRYOS™ YC-Style Stress Test

## Purpose

This is a brutal startup stress test for FOUNDRYOS Launcher.

It is designed to attack weak thinking before the market does.

Use it before investor conversations, sales demos, product launches, and template releases.

## The one brutal question

```text
Is this a real business, or just an impressive AI demo?
```

## 1. Problem test

### Questions

- What specific painful problem does FOUNDRYOS solve?
- Who has this problem badly enough to pay this month?
- What are they doing today instead?
- Is the problem urgent, expensive, frequent, and obvious?
- Would they pay before the product is perfect?

### Fail signal

The pitch sounds like:

```text
Everyone could use this.
```

That usually means nobody is desperate yet.

### Pass signal

The pitch sounds like:

```text
AI agency owners cannot productise delivery and are drowning in disconnected tools. We deploy a full AI company shell with GTM, proposal, invoice, delivery, and reporting in days.
```

## 2. Customer test

### Questions

- Who is the first buyer by name/type?
- Who owns budget?
- Who feels the pain?
- Who says yes?
- Who blocks it?
- Can we reach 100 of them this week?

### Initial best buyer

```text
AI agency / automation operator already selling services and needing productised company templates.
```

## 3. Wedge test

### Questions

- What is the first narrow wedge?
- Is it small enough to explain in 10 seconds?
- Is it big enough to expand into the broader vision?
- Can it produce a paid install?

### Recommended wedge

```text
Deploy an AI company for your agency or niche in one week.
```

Do not lead with:

```text
Universal autonomous venture foundry for every business on earth.
```

That is the big vision, not the wedge.

## 4. Demo test

### The demo must show

1. User describes company.
2. Launcher selects template.
3. Connector cockpit appears.
4. Agent org chart appears.
5. Revenue sprint appears.
6. Proposal/invoice workflow appears.
7. Approval gates appear.
8. Proof-of-work dashboard appears.

### Fail signal

The demo only shows a landing page and a plan.

### Pass signal

The demo shows a company package moving from concept to commercial action.

## 5. Revenue test

### Questions

- Can we earn before full autonomy?
- What can be sold this week?
- Is the buyer paying for software, setup, implementation, or transformation?
- What is the first paid pilot?

### First paid offer

```text
FOUNDRYOS Founder Install: $2,500
```

Deliver:

- deployed launcher,
- selected company template,
- agent org chart,
- skill pack,
- first revenue sprint,
- proposal/invoice workflow,
- approval gates.

## 6. Moat test

### Weak moat

```text
We use better prompts.
```

### Better moat

- Vertical company templates.
- Skill Foundry library.
- Operating history.
- Proof-of-work archive.
- Revenue benchmark data.
- Connector/harness layer.
- Marketplace distribution.
- Governance/audit layer.
- Customer-specific company packages.

## 7. Distribution test

### Questions

- What channel reaches desperate buyers today?
- Who already trusts us?
- What proof asset creates pull?
- What partner can resell this?

### First channels

- founder-led outreach,
- demos to AI/GHL agencies,
- hospitality consultant network,
- build-in-public proof,
- partner installs,
- template marketplace.

## 8. Pricing test

### Bad pricing

```text
$29/month for unlimited AI companies.
```

This destroys positioning and support capacity.

### Better pricing

```text
$2,500 founding install
$499-$2,500/month company lease
$297-$997 self-install template
$5,000-$25,000/month enterprise private foundry
```

## 9. Trust test

### Questions

- Can customers trust it with credentials?
- Can customers see what agents did?
- Can they approve risky actions?
- Can they stop/rollback workflows?
- Does it avoid sketchy automation?

### Required trust features

- approval gates,
- audit log,
- scoped credentials,
- official OAuth/API flows,
- proof-of-work artifacts,
- no hidden session extraction,
- no fake account/farm behaviour.

## 10. Investor test

### Questions

- Why is this venture-backable?
- Why does this become a platform?
- What repeats across customers?
- What compounds?
- What gets cheaper/better with usage?

### Investor answer

Each installed company creates reusable skills, templates, benchmarks, workflows, and proof data. Over time, FOUNDRYOS becomes a marketplace and operating layer for deployable AI companies.

## 11. Kill list

Kill anything that does not create:

- revenue,
- proof,
- template reuse,
- skill improvement,
- deployment speed,
- trust,
- or defensibility.

## 12. Weekly stress score

Score 1-5:

- Problem urgency
- Buyer clarity
- Wedge sharpness
- Demo credibility
- Revenue proximity
- Distribution access
- Pricing strength
- Trust/governance
- Moat compounding
- Investor narrative

Total:

```text
/50
```

### Score interpretation

```text
0-20: AI theatre
21-30: interesting prototype
31-40: sellable wedge
41-50: serious startup asset
```
